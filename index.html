The Origin
I was a creative. Then I
became a
creator.

If you've ever spent an entire afternoon perfecting the aesthetic philosophy of something you haven't actually started building yet, you'll know exactly where I began. I'm an artist who became a businessperson who became, apparently, a builder. For two decades, I knew what I wanted things to look and feel like. I had opinions about sacred geometry. I had a vision. What I didn't have was a way to get it from my head into the world without handing the keys to someone else. And every time I hired someone, I lost something. A little of the vision. A little of the control. And a lot of the feeling that this was actually mine. Maybe you know that feeling. That specific kind of disempowerment that comes from being someone who has the ideas but not the ability to build them on your own.

Then I moved to Guatemala. And somewhere between the lake, the mountains, and the community of dogs I'm mama to, something shifted. I stopped waiting to be ready and just started building. And something I can only call magic happened.

I realized I had access to something I never had before. Not just a tool. A collaborator. And with it, I was able to do things I genuinely didn't think were in my range. I am a coach, an artist, a facilitator, a writer. And now, apparently, also a developer.

As an artist, I've always known that building a universe means you also get to destroy it. You make something, you burn it down, you build something entirely new. Giving yourself that permission — to destroy your own work — is one of the hardest things you'll ever do. And one of the most important. This is just that, in a new medium.

And maybe it was never about being a tech person or not being a tech person. We are so quick to hand ourselves labels, and those labels quietly decide what we're capable of. Maybe I'm still not a tech person. Maybe I'm just a person who is no longer afraid of tech. A person who used it to put her entire life's creative work on a beautiful stage. Which, when I actually think about it, is the most creative thing I've ever done.

Some of us have a very wide range in life. The Renaissance Crew. The jack-of-all-trades who've always felt like they could do more than any one box would hold. This is the ultimate yes to that. It just expands the continuum of who you are. And that, to me, is magic.

I found something so incredible that I had to share it. That's always been why I teach. This is me finally being able to build the thing I'm teaching and teach it while I'm building it.

The Human Behind It

20 years of coaching, storytelling, and masterful listening.
AI is just the newest collaborator.

Here's what they're not telling you in those "build your website in three hours" videos: the tools don't do the work. You do. And what you bring to those tools is everything.

Two decades as an executive coach, a facilitator, a communicator, a storyteller. That's what was sitting across from the AI when I started building. The ability to listen deeply, to ask the right questions, to know what something needs to become. All of it became the creative director driving the technology. And that's the part no prompt can replace.

The first site took me a month and a half. It was slow, it was deep, and it was exactly what it needed to be. Then I cracked the system. The next five sites came in two weeks. When you have the right process and you finally trust yourself to use it, it gets faster and better and more exciting every single time. That's when I knew this had to be shared.

Your experience, your voice, your years of becoming who you are — that's the creative direction that makes everything you build with AI worth building. This portal is about how to use all of it.
